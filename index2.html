Animal Rights:

Animal rights have existed since the dawn of humanity.
Indian law has acknowledged animal rights since its inception, notably through the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act of 1960. Despite being decades old, this act still provides significant recognition to animal rights. Categorization of Animals:
The law categorizes animals into two groups: wild animals and domestic animals. Severe penalties, including the death penalty, are imposed under this act for offenses against animals. Reporting Crimes:
Individuals witnessing crimes against animals can report them to organizations like PETA. PETA is an organization established under the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act of 1960, offering a platform to report such incidents.
